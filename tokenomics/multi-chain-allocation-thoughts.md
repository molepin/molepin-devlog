# MOLEPIN Multi-Chain Allocation Thoughts

## Why MOLEPIN Was Designed as a Multi-Chain Token

From the beginning, MOLEPIN was never intended to exist on only one blockchain.

The idea was to build an ecosystem capable of evolving across multiple EVM-compatible chains while maintaining a unified global supply model.

Rather than treating chains as isolated ecosystems, I wanted MOLEPIN to move dynamically depending on:

- user activity
- liquidity flow
- ecosystem adoption
- bridge usage
- future infrastructure growth

---

# Initial Total Supply

Total Supply:

6,942,151,277,013 MOL

This number was intentionally fixed as the global maximum supply across all supported chains.

The supply itself was never intended to increase indefinitely.
Instead, the circulating distribution between chains could evolve over time through bridge-based mint/burn mechanics.

# Why These Ratios Were Considered

## Base (30%)

Base received the highest allocation because of:

- Coinbase ecosystem exposure
- strong onboarding potential
- rapid L2 ecosystem growth
- consumer-oriented infrastructure

The assumption was that Base could become a major gateway for mainstream users.

---

## Arbitrum One (25%)

Arbitrum was considered one of the strongest liquidity ecosystems among Ethereum L2 networks.

Key reasons:

- large TVL
- active DeFi ecosystem
- strong bridge activity
- mature developer ecosystem

---

## BNB Chain (15%)

BNB Chain was viewed as an important retail liquidity chain.

The allocation considered:

- high retail trading activity
- broad global accessibility
- meme token friendliness
- lower transaction costs

---

## zkSync Era (10%)

zkSync represented future-oriented infrastructure thinking.

This allocation reflected interest in:

- zk-rollup scalability
- account abstraction evolution
- future Ethereum scaling architecture

---

## Polygon (7%)

Polygon was intentionally assigned a moderate starting allocation.

Although Polygon had strong accessibility and mass adoption potential, the idea was to leave room for future dynamic liquidity movement between ecosystems.

---

## Optimism and Avalanche (5%)

These chains were included to maintain ecosystem diversity and reduce dependence on a single chain environment.

---

## Klaytn (3%)

Klaytn was considered from a regional perspective.

The allocation was small, but intentionally preserved because of Korean market familiarity and regional accessibility.

---

# Supply Flow Philosophy

One important realization during development was:

Initial allocation does not mean permanent liquidity dominance.

If users move assets through bridge systems, liquidity distribution can naturally shift over time.

For example:

- users may move from BNB → Polygon
- Polygon liquidity may eventually exceed BNB
- bridge mint/burn mechanics allow dynamic redistribution

This became one of the core design ideas behind the MOLEPIN bridge architecture.

---

# Mint/Burn Bridge Model

The long-term assumption was based on a lock/mint or burn/mint bridge structure.

Core concept:

- total supply remains fixed
- liquidity distribution changes dynamically
- tokens migrate depending on ecosystem demand

This means chain dominance is determined by actual usage, not only by the original allocation table.

---

# Important Note

These were early experimental ecosystem allocation thoughts during the design phase of MOLEPIN.

The architecture evolved continuously as bridge models, liquidity assumptions, and multi-chain infrastructure strategies changed.
